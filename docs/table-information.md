# Table Information Provided by High-End European Restaurants
### Comprehensive Details for Guest Reservations

When guests make a reservation at a fine-dining or luxury European restaurant, the establishment should provide clear, accurate, and detailed information about the **table** they will be assigned. This ensures transparency, comfort, and a premium experience aligned with high-end hospitality standards.

---

## 1. Table Location Details
### **1.1 Dining Area Placement**
- **Indoor / Outdoor** (weather-dependent options)
- **Main dining room / Secondary dining room**
- **Near window**, offering city view, garden view, sea view, or landmark view
- **Distance from high-traffic zones** (kitchen, bar, entrance, restroom)
- **Corner table** or **central table**
- **Quiet zone** vs. **lively area** depending on ambiance preferences

### **1.2 Atmospheric Conditions**
- Lighting level (soft, warm, candle-lit, natural daylight)
- Surrounding noise level (quiet, ambient music, live music nearby)
- Air conditioning or heating proximity
- Ventilation quality (for restaurants with open kitchens or terraces)

---

## 2. Table Specifications
### **2.1 Table Type and Size**
- Table for **2 / 4 / 6 / 8 / 10+**
- **Round**, **square**, or **rectangular** table
- **Table dimensions** (when applicable)
- **Table material** (wood, marble, glass) for guest preference

### **2.2 Seating Arrangement**
- Seating capacity (maximum and recommended)
- **Chair type** (standard, cushioned, armchair, banquet seating)
- **Space between tables** for privacy and comfort
- Possibility of **adding extra chairs** (if allowed)

---

## 3. Special Table Categories
### **3.1 VIP or Premium Tables**
- Location (exclusive section, private alcove, panoramic view)
- Minimum spend or **additional service charge** (if applicable)
- Enhanced privacy (curtains, partitions)

### **3.2 Private Dining Rooms**
- Seating capacity and room size
- Dedicated staff or butler service
- Audio/visual equipment availability
- Room usage fee or minimum spending requirement

### **3.3 Chef’s Table**
- Distance from kitchen or within kitchen
- Interaction with chef
- Tasting menu-only policy (if applicable)
- Special safety or dress-code rules

---

## 4. Reservation Timing and Table Availability
### **4.1 Reservation Timeframe**
- Confirmed **arrival time**
- **Grace period** before the table is released (commonly 10–20 minutes)

### **4.2 Table Usage Duration**
- Maximum seating time during peak hours (e.g., 2 hours)
- Flexible timing during off-peak periods
- Policies for late seating or extended stay

---

## 5. Table Preparation & Customization
### **5.1 Guest Requests**
- Seating preference (window, quiet corner, away from music)
- **High chair** or booster seat for children
- Accessibility needs (wheelchair-friendly tables)
- Dietary or cultural considerations for table setup

### **5.2 Special Occasion Setup**
- Birthday or anniversary decorations
- Customized floral arrangements
- Name cards or personalized menus
- Cake service arrangements and fees

---

## 6. Policies Related to the Table
### **6.1 Deposit or Prepayment**
- Required for:
    - Private dining rooms
    - High-demand dates (holidays, events)
    - Large groups

### **6.2 Cancellation Policy**
- Timeframe for free cancellation
- Penalties for late cancellation or no-show
- Refund policy for deposits

### **6.3 Upgrading or Changing the Table**
- Conditions for table changes upon arrival
- Possibility of upgrade (if available)
- Waiting time for specific table types

---

## 7. Facilities and Amenities Near the Table
- Restroom proximity
- Charging outlets availability
- Coat check or personal item storage
- Baby-changing area (for family-friendly restaurants)
- View of live entertainment (piano, violin, jazz band)

---

## 8. Additional Notes for High-End European Restaurants
- Some restaurants enforce **dress code** tied to certain table areas (e.g., jacket required for main dining room)
- Certain tables may require specific **menu formats** (chef’s tasting menu, wine pairing)
- Seasonal terrace tables may depend on weather conditions and may be subject to last-minute changes

---

## Summary
Luxury European restaurants should offer guests precise, comprehensive table information to ensure a high-quality dining experience. Transparency about location, table features, timing, customization options, and policies elevates service standards and aligns with the expectations of fine-dining clientele.
